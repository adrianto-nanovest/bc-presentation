# Monthly Review Process Manual v2.1

## Purpose

This manual defines the standard process for conducting a monthly operational review. Its goal is to
ensure that performance data from the previous month is assessed systematically, that deviations from
target are traced to root causes, and that findings reach decision-makers in a form they can act on.
The review closes with a signed-off report and an action list that has a named owner for every item.

---

## Who runs the review

**Section Head** — responsible for analysis and insight.
- Reads and interprets the 6-tab workbook (Schema + 5 data tabs).
- Investigates incidents and vendor issues that appear in the data.
- Drafts the monthly report and the executive slide deck.
- Makes a recommendation on the key deviation(s).
- Signs off the report before distribution.

**Team Leader** — responsible for execution and team coordination.
- Confirms the accuracy of shift logs and workbook inputs before review begins.
- Tracks open actions from the previous month; marks any as Closed or Escalated.
- Compiles the shift handover record and the weekly recap.
- Signs off the action list.

---

## Required data inputs

The review requires the following starter-pack files to be complete before the session begins:

1. **Monthly Ops Workbook** (6 tabs):
   - Schema — field-level documentation for all data tabs
   - Tab 1 — Daily output log
   - Tab 2 — Shift schedule
   - Tab 3 — Incidents log
   - Tab 4 — Vendor deliveries
   - Tab 5 — KPI targets vs actuals
2. **Incident Brief** — narrative writeup for any incident flagged for root-cause review.
3. **Vendor Notice(s)** — formal communication from any vendor with a delivery issue.
4. **Report Template** — skeleton document; Section Head fills during review.
5. **Slide Template** — 8-slide deck spec; Section Head populates for exec briefing.
6. **Site Snapshots** — any photo or field notes referenced in the incidents log.

If any required input is missing, the Section Head escalates to whoever owns that input before
proceeding. The review does not begin with incomplete data.

---

## Review cadence

- Reviews run **monthly**, during **Week 1 of the following month**.
- Typical timeline:

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Team Leader confirms workbook inputs are complete and accurate. |
| Day 2 | Section Head reads workbook; flags deviations and anomalies. |
| Day 3 | Root-cause session — Section Head investigates flagged items; Team Leader provides context. |
| Day 4 | Section Head drafts report and slide deck. |
| Day 5 | Both roles review draft; sign-off. Distribution same day. |

Urgent deviations (see Escalation Rules) do not wait for the monthly cycle — they escalate immediately.

---

## Escalation rules

Escalate **immediately** (do not wait for next review cycle) when any of the following are true:

1. **Critical incident** — any incident logged as "Critical" in Tab 3, or any cluster of ≥ 2 Medium
   incidents within a 3-day window affecting the same system.
2. **Vendor dispute** — a vendor disputes a delivery shortfall in writing, or has a documented pattern
   of ≥ 3 partial deliveries in a rolling 90-day period.
3. **KPI variance > 10%** — any single KPI in Tab 5 shows actual more than 10% below target for the
   period. Volume and Yield variances take priority.
4. **Unresolved incident lineage** — an incident in the current month is linked by root-cause to an
   event from a prior month that was not fully investigated at the time.

Escalation goes to the Section Head immediately; Section Head escalates further to the relevant
approver per the organisational escalation matrix.

---

## Expected outputs

**Section Head produces:**
- Monthly Report (filled report template) — findings, evidence table, recommendations.
- Executive Slide Deck (8 slides) — cover, headline metric, driver analysis, incidents recap,
  vendor status, KPI scorecard, action items, sign-off.
- Decision Recommendation — 1-page or embedded in report executive summary.

**Team Leader produces:**
- Shift Handover Record — summary of handover status at month close.
- Status Update — brief written update on the previous month's open action items.
- Action List — named actions from this review, each with owner and due date.
- Weekly Recap — one-paragraph summary per week of the review month.

Both roles are expected to use an AI-augmented workflow for drafting, evidence organisation, and
root-cause analysis. AI-generated content must be reviewed and verified against source data before
sign-off.

---

## Sign-off

- The **Section Head** signs off the Report and Slide Deck.
- The **Team Leader** signs off the Action List.
- **Both signatures are required** before the review is considered closed and outputs are distributed.
- Sign-off date is recorded in the Report template (cover page, Date field) and on the Action List.
