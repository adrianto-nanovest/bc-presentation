# Incident Brief — April 28, 2026

**Reference:** INC-2026-0428
**Severity at time of logging:** Non-critical (reclassified — see below)
**Prepared by:** I. Pratiwi, Shift Supervisor, Receiving Team
**Date prepared:** April 29, 2026

---

## Summary

On April 28, 2026, the receiving team accepted an inbound shipment from vendor Delta Pratama that
carried inconsistent batch markings on a subset of packaged components. The discrepancy was assessed
at the time as a minor administrative variance and the shipment was accepted in full. Subsequent
handling and distribution of those components into the working inventory was not flagged, and no
hold was placed on the batch. The issue re-manifested during May 14–17, when three Critical incidents
were logged in the operations area — all traceable, by component serial and batch reference, to
material drawn from this April shipment.

## Evidence gaps

## Initial assessment (at the time)

Classified on April 28 as: *"Non-critical material variance, contained — label formatting only,
no quality impact."* No escalation triggered; no follow-up action assigned.

## Why this is being re-examined

The cluster of three Critical incidents logged May 15–17 (see Tab 3 — incidents log, rows for
2026-05-15, 2026-05-16, and 2026-05-17) has been provisionally traced, via component batch
reference, to material drawn from the April 28 Delta Pratama shipment. The May 14 partial delivery
shortfall (see Tab 4 and Vendor Notice 04) compounded the situation by reducing the buffer of
good-quality components. Root-cause investigation for the May incidents must reconnect to this
April event and determine whether the April 28 classification ("administrative variance") was
correct, and whether the acceptance decision contributed to the May outcomes.
