# AI Landscape Paradox — Global Research
## For B.5 Slide | May 2026

---

## 1. The Paradox in One Sentence

Organizations are adopting AI at record speed—**88% use AI in at least one business function**—yet **only 5.5% see measurable ROI**, and **42% of companies abandon most AI initiatives** before extracting value. The gap is not capability; it is **fundamental understanding**: people are reaching for AI tools without grasping what they can/cannot do, how to evaluate outputs, or how to structure data and workflows around them.

## 3. The Root Cause: Fundamentals Gap

The paradox is not driven by technology maturity, cost constraints, or lack of investment. It reflects **organizational and individual lack of foundational AI understanding**.

### Evidence Supporting the Fundamentals Thesis

#### **Finding 1: Low AI Literacy Among Daily Users**
- 73% of knowledge workers use AI tools at least weekly, but only **29% self-rate as "advanced"** in AI literacy.
- Only **35% of organizations** have a **mature, organization-wide AI upskilling program**, despite 77% offering *some* form of training.
- **Gap**: Training exists but is ad hoc; it does not build cumulative, fundamental understanding.
- *Source*: DataCamp *State of Data & AI Literacy 2026*; https://www.datacamp.com/blog/the-state-of-data-and-ai-literacy-in-2026-definitions-statistics-and-the-ai-skills-gap

#### **Finding 2: Executives Cannot Evaluate Outputs**
- Data leaders rank **"interpreting AI outputs"** and **"turning information into sound decisions"** as the **top two capability shortfalls**.
- 59% of data leaders report an **AI skills gap** within their organization, even though they lead data functions.
- **Gap**: Access to AI tools ≠ ability to validate or act on AI-generated results.
- *Source*: DataCamp / Medium analysis *2026 Surveys Expose Data Leaders Skills Crisis*; https://medium.com/@vikramlingam/2026-surveys-expose-data-leaders-skills-crisis-in-ai-output-interpretation-3-mar-2026

#### **Finding 3: Structured Training Doubles ROI**
- Organizations **without** mature AI upskilling programs: **21% report significant ROI**.
- Organizations **with** mature programs: **42% report significant ROI**.
- **Ratio**: ~2x improvement with proper foundational training.
- **Implication**: Organizations that invest in fundamentals (not just tool access) see dramatically better value realization.
- *Source*: DataCamp *State of Data & AI Literacy 2026*; https://www.datacamp.com/blog/the-state-of-data-and-ai-literacy-in-2026-definitions-statistics-and-the-ai-skills-gap

#### **Finding 4: Job Redesign Lags Adoption by 84%**
- 82% of organizations expect **at least 10% job automation** within 3 years.
- Yet **84% have not redesigned jobs** around AI capabilities.
- **Gap**: Orgs are adopting AI without reimagining work flows, governance, or decision-making processes around AI's strengths/limitations.
- *Source*: Deloitte *State of AI in the Enterprise 2026*; https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/what-we-do/capabilities/applied-artificial-intelligence/content/state-of-ai-in-the-enterprise.html

#### **Finding 5: Insufficient Worker Skills Are the #1 Barrier**
- When asked about barriers to integrating AI, leaders cite **"insufficient worker skills"** as the **single biggest obstacle**—ahead of data quality, cost, or governance.
- **Gap**: The foundational gap is understood by leadership but not being addressed at scale.
- *Source*: Deloitte *State of AI in the Enterprise 2026* (survey of 3,235 global leaders, Aug–Sep 2025); https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/what-we-do/capabilities/applied-artificial-intelligence/content/state-of-ai-in-the-enterprise.html
